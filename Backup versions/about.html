About International Business Conferences

21 years. 1,500+ conferences. One mission.

Since 2001, IBC has been India's most consistent convening platform for industry practitioners — a place where the people running operations across pharma, safety, HR, manufacturing, and infrastructure meet, learn, and lead.

Our Origin

Founded in 2001. Built one conference at a time.

IBC was founded in September 2001 by Rahul Shah, in Mumbai, with a simple premise: India's industry leaders needed a serious place to meet that wasn't a trade show.

Twenty-one years later, we run conferences, awards, and roundtables across pharma, safety, HR, manufacturing, real estate, and infrastructure. We've trained operating teams at ONGC, Aurobindo Pharma, SRF, Reliance Energy, and Vedanta. We've convened more than 3,500 speakers and 1,500 conferences. Our advisory board carries over 350 years of combined operating experience.

What we do has expanded. Why we do it hasn't.

Mission & Vision

What we're working toward

Two ways of stating the same intent — what we do every day, and where we want that to lead.

Our Mission

To give India's senior practitioners a serious, evidence-led place to meet — where agendas are built from real operating problems, not buzzwords, and every speaker has actually done the work.

Our Vision

To be India's most trusted convening platform for industry — the first call for any practitioner, regulator, or operator who wants a room of peers, not a trade show.

How We Work

Three principles, not a slogan

No mnemonic, no acronym — just how a programme actually gets built.

We programme around practitioners, not platforms.

Our agendas are built by talking to operating teams about what they actually need to discuss — not assembled from industry buzzwords.

Our speakers have done the work.

Every flagship conference is anchored by speakers and advisors who have run the function, not just written about it. Our advisory board carries 350+ years of operating experience across HSSE, pharma R&D, electrical engineering, contract risk, and plant operations.

Our format follows the conversation.

A regulatory update is a webinar. A senior cross-industry discussion is a roundtable. A skills programme is an in-house cohort. We pick the format that fits the brief.
