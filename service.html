A2 / Workshops & Seminars

Workshops & Seminars

Focused, single-topic sessions for skill-building and practical application, usually one or two days.

Workshops are smaller and more hands-on than conferences. They run as single-topic sessions over one or two days — case studies, structured exercises, and group discussion. Each is led by a subject-matter expert with operating experience in the field, not a generalist trainer. Recent examples include "Turn Your L&D Team into AI-Powered Creators," delivered for Cloudthat in 2026, and "Nuts and Bolts of Construction Safety," a two-day workshop paired with our construction safety conference.

A3 / Webinars

Webinars

Shorter online sessions on time-sensitive topics, usually with a small interactive panel.

Webinars are how we run the conversations that don't need a room. A regulatory update, a function-specific deep dive, a panel between three or four practitioners — each runs live, then stays available on demand. The format suits topics where speed matters more than scale.

B1 / Bespoke Roundtables

Bespoke Roundtables

Closed-door, curated conversations between 15–25 senior decision-makers, built around a specific industry question.

A bespoke roundtable is a small, invitation-only event built around one substantive question — usually for a corporate or industry body that wants to host a senior conversation without organising it themselves. IBC handles the brief, the curation, the invitations, the venue, the moderation, and the post-event documentation.

  • Proven track record across PSUs, corporates, and government organisations.
  • Direct access to senior decision-makers, subject-matter experts, and key stakeholders.
  • Curated content delivered by industry advisors with 30+ years of operating experience.
  • High brand visibility through co-branding, promotions, and on-ground presence.
  • End-to-end execution: brief, curation, venue, hosting, documentation.

Event Planning & Management

C1 / In-house Training

In-house Training

Private training programs delivered inside client organisations, designed around their specific operating context.

In-house training is the most direct way to work with us. We come to your team — at your office, your plant, or a venue you choose — and run a program built around your operating context. Each program is led by an advisor with direct industry experience: process safety led by our former BPCL Corporate HSSE Head, plant maintenance by an ex-IOCL Chief General Manager, contract risk by a strategic contract-risk consultant with 30 years of practice. We don't deploy generalist trainers.

Most engagements follow the same structure: a scoping conversation, a site visit or operating review, a custom content build, in-person delivery, and post-training follow-up. Duration ranges from a one-day session to a multi-month cohort.
